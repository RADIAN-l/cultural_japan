Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice accompanied by a variety of ingredients, such as seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits.

  • Made with specially prepared rice seasoned with vinegar
  • Features fresh fish, vegetables, and other ingredients
  • Various types: Nigiri, Maki, Sashimi, and more
  • Often served with wasabi, ginger, and soy sauce

Best Seasons

Available year-round, but certain fish are best in specific seasons (e.g., fatty tuna in winter)

Ramen

Ramen is a noodle soup dish that consists of Chinese wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso.

  • Four main soup bases: Shoyu, Miso, Shio, and Tonkotsu
  • Toppings include chashu pork, soft-boiled eggs, and vegetables
  • Noodles vary in thickness and texture
  • Popular comfort food, especially in cold weather

Best Seasons

Perfect for cold weather, but enjoyed year-round. Different broths suit different seasons.

Tempura

Tempura is a dish of battered and deep-fried seafood, meat, and vegetables. The batter is made of cold water and wheat flour, sometimes with egg.

  • Light, crispy batter that doesn't absorb much oil
  • Common ingredients: shrimp, fish, eggplant, sweet potato
  • Served with tentsuyu dipping sauce
  • Portuguese influence from the 16th century

Best Seasons

Available year-round, but seasonal vegetables make it special in spring and autumn

Miso Soup

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup consisting of a stock called "dashi" into which softened miso paste is mixed. It's a staple of Japanese cuisine.

  • Made with fermented soybean paste (miso)
  • Contains dashi stock, tofu, and various vegetables
  • Different types of miso for different flavors
  • Often served as part of a traditional breakfast

Best Seasons

Enjoyed year-round, with seasonal vegetables and ingredients changing with the seasons

Takoyaki are ball-shaped Japanese snacks made of a wheat flour-based batter and cooked in a special molded pan. They are typically filled with minced or diced octopus.

  • Ball-shaped snacks with octopus filling
  • Topped with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, and bonito flakes
  • Popular street food, especially in Osaka
  • Often served at festivals and street markets

Best Seasons

Popular year-round, but especially enjoyed during summer festivals and street food events

Wagyu Beef

ÂíåÁâõ

Wagyu refers to several breeds of cattle genetically predisposed to intense marbling and to producing a high percentage of oleaginous unsaturated fat.

  • Intense marbling creates melt-in-your-mouth texture
  • Several grades: A1-A5, with A5 being the highest
  • Famous varieties: Kobe, Matsusaka, and Ohmi
  • Often served as steak, sukiyaki, or shabu-shabu

Best Seasons

Available year-round, but autumn and winter are considered ideal for rich beef dishes
